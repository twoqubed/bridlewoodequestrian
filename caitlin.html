Caitlin Ader







Caitlin Ader has owned and cared for ponies and horses almost her entire life. She grew up around her mother’s horses, and began formal riding lessons on her first pony when she was six years old. A year later, she started her junior career, training and showing with renowned professionals, most notably Christina Schlusemeyer and Don Stewart, Jr., top equitation and jumper trainers in Ocala, Florida. Caitlin attended private school through the 8th grade, and then completed her education in an online environment so as to have the flexibility to maintain her rigorous training and showing schedule.

All during her junior years, Caitlin competed successfully in United States Equestrian Federation AA rated horse shows up and down the east coast including East Dorset, Vermont, Lake Placid and Saugerties, New York, Lexington, Kentucky, Lexington, Virginia, Atlanta, Georgia and Ocala and Wellington, Florida.

After completing her high school education, Caitlin attended Texas Christian University in Fort Worth, Texas, graduating in May 2014. While at TCU she was a vital member of the varsity NCEA equestrian team - a regular on the starting roster competing in almost every meet.

During her four years in college, Caitlin received academic achievement regard, was a multiple varsity letter award winner, two-time All-American victor and earned several Most Outstanding Player honors in both equitation over fences and on the flat. In addition, Caitlin was esteemed with becoming the first student-athlete in TCU's history to earn the Big 12 conference First Team honors in both equitation over fences and on the flat, and also only the second student-athlete to ever claim All-American recognition in equitation on the flat.

Upon graduation, Caitlin moved to Massachusetts, taking on an assistant trainer and professional rider position and then accepted a barn manager opportunity with a well-known show barn in Westchester County, New York.

Caitlin took on the role of the manager of Bridlewood Stables and Equestrian Center in January 2016.
